Exploring the Future of Digital Public Services

The Client

A central government programme focused on transforming how people interact with public services.

The Challenge

The client had developed a bold vision for the future of digital government—but needed help visualising what that future might look and feel like for real people: citizens, public servants, frontline workers, and business owners.

Our Approach

Working as part of a multidisciplinary design team, we led a period of rapid exploration, sketching, prototyping, and storytelling. This wasn’t about speculative design—it was grounded, iterative, and focused on what it would take to make this future real.

We developed a series of design artefacts:
  • Scenario-based storyboards showing how services might anticipate user needs
  • Sketches of digital infrastructure and its support for joined-up delivery
  • A prototype site to test and share ideas internally
The Outcome

The work helped build alignment and momentum across the wider transformation programme. The idea of “services doing more for users” started to feel less like a distant ambition and more like something teams could actively design for.

Making Complex Services Understandable

The Client

A major cross-government digital identity programme enabling quick, secure access to public services.

The Challenge

As the service grew complex, existing tools were insufficient for teams to explore and understand it. We needed a solution that was easy to use, explorable, and low maintenance.

Our Approach

We designed an interactive HTML prototype using the GOV.UK Prototype Kit, with three guiding principles:

  • Make it realistic – match the real user experience.
  • Keep it simple – easy to explore and update.
  • Start small and iterate – focus on key journeys and expand.

We focused on creating a scenario-based prototype, clear design language, flexible navigation, accessible versions, and a federated update model via GitHub.

The Outcome

The tool became essential for alignment and onboarding across the programme. It’s now used by:

  • Service teams to explore integration without technical access.
  • Contact centre staff to reduce query resolution time.
  • Designers and researchers to test realistic journeys.
  • Product managers to drive a more pattern-based approach.
  • Engineers to understand flow logic and test ideas.

What started as a simple prototype evolved into a trusted, living documentation platform, helping all teams understand the bigger picture.

Designing for Inclusion in Digital Identity

The Client

A cross-government digital identity programme enabling people to prove their identity to access vital public services online.

The Challenge

Millions in the UK struggle to prove their identity online due to lack of digital literacy, conventional ID, or stable addresses. This exclusion affects hundreds of thousands, hindering access to essential government services. The client sought to design inclusive, scalable solutions to reduce reliance on offline alternatives like contact centres while meeting security requirements.

Our Approach

We explored how digital identity could work for people with low digital literacy or without conventional IDs. Our research focused on alternative ID sources and users' comfort with verifying their identity online. We prioritised five data sources—banking, digital vouching, NHS, DWP, and General Registry Office records—and ran time-boxed experiments to identify viable solutions.

The Solution

We prototyped three key opportunities for inclusion:

  • Digital vouching – Peer verification through trusted individuals, showing strong user acceptance.
  • Security questions – Dynamic, user-specific security questions for adaptable verification based on risk.
  • Joining up government data – Proposals to use verified government data across services with user consent.
The Outcome

This work laid the foundation for an inclusive, adaptable digital identity service. Service design now considers a broader range of user circumstances, offline support options are being developed, and new approaches are being trialled as part of the platform roadmap.
